How to download an iso

Congrats! You have now decided on your distro of choice! Now what?

Well, you need to get an iso. There are different methods to getting one and it's important we go over some language: mirror and torrent. Let's go from easiest to hardest.

What's a mirror?

A mirror is what you may expect when you typically download something. You click a link, click download, and it's downloaded.

If you can do this, you're good to move onto the next step

What's a torrent? Isn't torrenting illegal?

A torrent is to put it simply, where you use a torrenting app to ask to download a file by borrowing a copy from someone that's decided to share it.

Now torrenting isn't illegal. However, torrenting pirated copies of something (which torrenting became famous for) is. Thankfully though, many Linux developers don't quite care about you making additional copies of their software and actively encourage you spread their amazing, completely free code everywhere.

To start, you need a torrenting application. For my example, I will use Transmission , simply because that's what I already have experience with. Just follow the setup process and you should be good to go.

All you need to do once you have your torrenting application and set the file you wish to use. If you are using Transmission, you can do the following steps

  1. Open Transmission
  2. Click file
  3. Click open
  4. Choose your torrent file
  5. Set your destination folder
  6. Set additional settings if you wish
  7. Click open
