What's a Distro?

Well, if you want it put simply, a distro is a different variation of Linux. Ok, if that's all you're looking for, you can go to the next page.

But maybe that simple explanation still leaves you with some questions as to how Linux really works. In that case, I can show you.

Think of Linux as a cocao bean. A Cocao bean is vastly different than any store bought chocolate you've ever seen. This is because a Cocao bean itself isn't really practical for an end consumer like you, but a more finished chocolate is.

Although all chocolate is made of cocao, not all chocolate may be the same. There's white, dark and milk, not to mention bitter bakers varieties among others. I wouldn't recommend to a first time chocolate eater a baker's Chocolate or white chocolate for example, because they are rather uninviting and wouldn't leave a good impression of "chocolate" with you. Linux is rather similar in that regard, although with far more white and absurdly dark varieties and it can be rather easy to pick those when you don't know your chocolates that well.

This section of the guide is designed to help you choose your first chocolate. Remember your first chocolate isn't likely to be your favorite, or even one you like. The good news is that Linux often allows you to sample distros before you do irreversable things to your hard drive so go out there and feel free to sample as many as you like.

To help you get started though (and avoid the bakers chocolate of the Linux world), we will cover what to look for on the next page.
