What's a Distro?

Well, if you want it put simply, a distro is a different variation of Linux. Ok, if that's all you're looking for, you can go to the next page.

But maybe that simple explanation still leaves you with some questions as to how Linux really works. In that case, I can show you.

Think of Linux as a cocao bean. If you have ever seen a cocao bean, you will see they are vastly different than any Hersheys or Milky Way you've seen in the store. This is because chefs, or developers in this case, prepare cocao before handing you a finished chocolate bar.

What people do with their cocao bean cooking can greatly differ though. While Cocao is incredibly customizable and can be used for a variety of tasks, most people just use it to make chocolate, although that's not to say chocolate can't be wildly different. I'm talking about the ultra-simplistic Baker's Chocolate, white chocolate, and all the variations of chocolate with nuts and carmel and the like you find at the grocery store.

Linux is rather similar in that regard. Lots of what people refer to as Linux is the chocolate distro layer that's far abstracted away from the cocao beans. That can mean different people like their Linux distros in different ways. Some like the bitterness of something difficult to use like Arch Linux while others like the simple stuff like Debian, one of the Hershey's of the Linux world. Others felt like it was missing some nuts like Ubuntu.

This section of the guide is designed to help you choose your first peice of chocolate so you don't end up eating a baker's chocolate and think it's disgusting. On the next page, we will help you pick things you may want in your first chocolate.
