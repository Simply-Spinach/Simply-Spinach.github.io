About this guide

When it comes to learning Linux, now has got to be one of the greatest times in history to experience it. Many distributions have became easy to use (many requiring very little terminal usage, if at all to use them) and there are far fewer leaps each year in computer speeds and technology each year, using a computer a bit longer is far more viable than it ever was before.

That's why I created this guide to help you get into a Linux environment.

This guide is designed for everyone, whether you be someone who doesn't know computers well or someone who has used computers their entire lives. From shopping to installing and resources to make the switch,

Topics this book will cover:

  • What is Linux?
  • What is a Linux distro and why are there so many?
  • How do I download and install this?
  • What am I supposed to expect at the end of this anyway?

Feel free to skip to any section of the book through the navigation on the left as needed. Be expected to know some parts or nothing at all. And above all, feel free to explore beyond just this resource, ask a friend, or google when you don't understand something.

And when it comes to learning Linux, the hardest obstacle is getting it installed. Feel free to ask a techy friend for help or ask around on the internet for further help if you need it.
