Running Linux for the First Time

At this point, your computer is ready to boot into Linux!

To do this, you're going to do one last final check on your data. If you haven't yet, back it up. Copy and paste your user folder into an external hard drive or something. You don't want to loose your stuff in the move.

And we are going to be turning off and on your computer yet again. Here's a QR code to get back here on your phone quickly.

Alright. Are you ready?

You're going to do something like we did with the BIOS (although I would be very impressed if you broke everything here). Turn off your computer and as you're turning it on, press the F12 key as fast as you can (remember to hold Fn if your computer needs that) (if that's the wrong key, look up how to do it on your computer and try again). This should open the boot menu. From here, use your arrow keys to select your flash drive and press enter.

Your computer will now open to whatever bootloader your distribution uses. Typically, this is Grub, which looks a lot like your BIOS, except it's all in black and white. Use the arrow keys and enter to select your OS and you should be booting. (If your computer somehow didn't end up here, then you have a BIOS problem and you should disable secure boot if you haven't yet, or you have some other problem you're going to need to look up somewhere else).

When you get here, you should see a bunch of text going quickly across your screen followed by your distribution logo.

That's it! You're Linux system is booting on your computer!

While I would love to help you from here, every distribution is different on the installation process once you actually get into it but if you use any of the distros I recommend for beginners , you should be pretty well handled from here by your computer.
