A story written by Caelin Conner
There once was a boy that drifted alone among the stars. He never saw anything beyond the stars and longed to find something more to his life. But he never saw much beyond himself. He asked an asteroid that passed by him.
"I'm missing something in life" said the boy.
"Do you have any friends?" asked the asteroid.
"No. Do you?"
"But there are many stars in the sky" said the asteroid. "There's a trillions of stars in the sky. Surely you will find some stars you enjoy. I'm currently looking for a star who's going to take me in."
"I surely will." Said the boy, a faint glimmer of hope in his eyes. Thank you asteroid."
So he searched out a star.
"Hello star" said the boy.
"Hello there" said the star back.
"I'm looking for somewhere to be"
"Well you can stay here" said the star. "There's plenty of room to orbit me".
So the boy did, around and around but he still felt lonely. The star barely interacted with him as he did so.
"Why don't you talk that much to me?" asked the boy.
"Well, there's a lot of bigger planets around me too. I just don't have time for you" said the star. "But I will talk to you later and then you won't be lonely."
But the star never did and the boy left.
But there are many more stars right? He went from one star, to the next and the next, but none appealed to him.
There was nothing there but he could feel he was being pulled in by something. A possible friend? He watched as the stars disappeared in front of him. They slowly warped around a dark black hole. He stared at the abscense, trying his best to comprehend it.
"Whatcha staring at boy?" snarks the black hole. "There's nothing out here. Go home".
"I don't have a home. I'm looking for friends"
"What a pitty" the black hole then procceeds to eat an entire star in it's enirety, sending a tremmor down the boy's back.
"Would you like to be my friend?"
"Absolutely not boy."
The boy escaped as fast as he could.

He couldn't find any friends. No matter how much the boy tried, but the boy never found anywhere he belonged.

The stars in the sky were so far away that their fiery furnaces couldn't keep his body warm. He kept himself away from the neverending hunger of the black holes but there was still nothing for him.

He never had a chance of finding a place he belonged. So he curled up into a ball, keeping what little warmth he had left from escaping into the rest of the universe.

Just as the boy gave up, a metal object flew next to him. It was different, searching for something, anything in the void.

"Who are you?" asked the boy.
"I'm Voyager 1" it says excitedly. "I'm from a planet called Earth."
"You probably don't know much about making friends..."
"I actually do! I'm from a planet full of people who make friends!" the ball then proceeds to play a record, playing beautiful sounds the boy has never heard before. The boy smiles as the wonderful tune passes by him.
"I came from that star over there" says the orb. "You can find Earth in the habitable zone."
"Well, it's still far away. I'm never going to reach Earth."
"I believe in you" said the Voyager. "I came out all this way all on my own".
The boy smiled.
"I'm going to keep exploring the universe beyond but I hope you enjoy your journey to toward Earth." The explorer then leaves, continuing it's journey into the unknown universe around him.
Enticed by the warm melody of the robot's tune, the boy went. Out of all the places he has been before, nobody wanted him but the people of Earth did. And after all of that, he was only a relatively short travel considering the age of the lights around him. His fingers, once numb and tingly became warmer and warmer as he passed by the relatively hostile planets of this system, but he didn't care about the many meteors he traveled past, he didn't care about how strong the gravity was from Jupyter's pull or being pelted by meteors in the meteor belt. When he reached Earth, he found the place he belonged and would not want to miss anywhere else in the universe to be because out of everywhere else in the world-
nobody else wanted him like Earth did
